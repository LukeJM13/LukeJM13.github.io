Invdev

March 2023 - Present

I started at Invdev in March 2023 as an Apprentice Software Developer. I knew the basics of HTML and CSS, as well as the bare minimum Javascript knowledge. Over the course of my apprenticeship I grew as a developer and built-out my list of tools and skills.
My main focus was on gaining an understanding of modern web application development, becoming familiar with React and Node.js, using Typescript/Javascript as my day-to-day coding language.
Along the way I spent time learning lots of other interesting skills, such as GraphQL, Docker, Cypress, Jest and Drupal, as well as the developer fundamentals like Git and GitHub.
I even got to work with exciting new technologies; Robotic Process Automation tools like UiPath and n8n, as well as low-code solutions such as Microsoft Power Platform.

Electric Car Scheme

August 2022 - December 2022

My time at The Electric Car Scheme, despite being quite short, was very helpful to my career.
For one thing it taught me that I'm probably not a natural salesperson and I would find more enjoyment in a career path better suited to me and my talents.
It was while working as a Business Development Executive at ECS that I decided to pursue a career in software development, which is something I'd always considered.
Sometimes it takes working a job that you don't enjoy to push you in the right direction. I will always be thankful for everyone at ECS for helping me discover my true passion and career path.

Bulb Energy

November 2019 - April 2022

I consider my time working at Bulb to be the most important period in my life for career, as well as personal growth.
Joining as an Energy Specialist and being tasked with speaking to up to 60 customers a day via phones, email and online chat really helped me grow in confidence and responsibility.
Spending time in the Social and Community team, conversing with customers via social media channels and managing the company app store accounts gave me a different perspective on B2C communications .
I also gained so much from my time as acting team leader, having to be responsible for a team of people and making sure to give them what they needed to help them grow professionally.
